## 💡 RECOMMENDED SALARY STRATEGY

### Option 1: Conservative (Recommended)

**Offer:**
- Base: RM 6,000/month
- Benefits: Medical (RM 500), Dental (RM 300), 16 days AL, Hybrid
- **6-month review clause:** If MRR > RM 15,000, salary increases to RM 6,500
- **12-month review clause:** If MRR > RM 25,000, salary increases to RM 7,500

**Why this works:**
- Low barrier to entry (easier for employer to say yes)
- Built-in incentive alignment (you win when they win)
- Predictable escalation path

---

### Option 2: Performance-Based

**Offer:**
- Base: RM 5,000/month (fixed)
- Commission: 5% of monthly fan club revenue
- Benefits: Medical, Dental, 16 days AL, Hybrid

**Example earnings:**
- Month 1 (MRR: RM 0): RM 5,000
- Month 3 (MRR: RM 8,500): RM 5,000 + RM 425 = RM 5,425
- Month 6 (MRR: RM 19,500): RM 5,000 + RM 975 = RM 5,975
- Month 12 (MRR: RM 30,000): RM 5,000 + RM 1,500 = **RM 6,500**

**Why this works:**
- Lowers risk for employer (fixed cost is only RM 5,000)
- Higher upside for you if you exceed targets
- Proves your confidence in revenue projections

---

### Option 3: Aggressive

**Offer:**
- Base: RM 7,000/month
- Benefits: Medical, Dental, 18 days AL, Hybrid, RM 500/month phone allowance
- No commission

**Why this might backfire:**
- Entertainment industry typically pays less than pure tech
- Small company may not have budget
- Employer might counter with RM 5,500, wasting negotiation capital

**Risk:** 40% chance of rejection or lowball counter

---

## 🎯 FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### Start with Option 1 (Conservative + Escalation)

**Your opening offer:**

> "I'm proposing a base salary of **RM 6,000/month** with standard benefits (medical, dental, 16 days annual leave, hybrid work). I'd like to include a **performance-based escalation clause**: if I achieve a Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) of RM 15,000 within 6 months, my salary increases to **RM 6,500**. If I hit RM 25,000 MRR within 12 months, it increases to **RM 7,500**."

**Why this wins:**

1. ✅ **Initial ask (RM 6,000) is defensible** (middle of market range)
2. ✅ **Escalation shows confidence** (you believe in your projections)
3. ✅ **Aligns incentives** (you only get raises if company succeeds)
4. ✅ **Gives employer control** (they see results before paying more)
5. ✅ **Room for negotiation** (if they counter at RM 5,500, you meet at RM 5,750)

## 🚨 Red Flags to Watch For

### Signs They're Lowballing You

1. **"We'll give you RM 4,000 and see how it goes"**
   - ❌ This is 33% below market—walk away
   - They don't value your skills

2. **"We can't afford benefits"**
   - ❌ Medical/EPF/SOCSO are standard even at small companies
   - Sign of financial instability

3. **"Work for equity instead of salary"**
   - ❌ Only acceptable if you're a co-founder
   - Entertainment companies rarely have viable exit strategies

4. **"Prove yourself for 3 months unpaid first"**
   - ❌ Illegal under Malaysian Employment Act
   - Report to labor department if suggested

---

### Signs of a Good Employer

1. ✅ "RM 6,000 is fair, let's discuss benefits"
2. ✅ "Can we do RM 5,500 with a 3-month review?"
3. ✅ "We like the performance bonus idea"
4. ✅ "What do you need to succeed in this role?"
